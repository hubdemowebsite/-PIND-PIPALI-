Our Story

From a Ludhiana Courtyard
to Your Table.

In the heart of Punjab, there is a rhythm to the seasons. In Ludhiana, when the summer sun hits its peak, the ritual begins. It starts with the selection of the finest Ramkela mangoes and the hand-pounding of sun-dried spices.

Pind & Pipali wasn't born in a factory; it was born in a sun-drenched courtyard, under the watchful eyes of a matriarch who knew that a perfect pickle cannot be rushed.

While the world moved toward mass production and chemical preservatives, we chose to stay behind. We stayed in the "Pind" while using "Pipali" to bridge the gap between tradition and the modern gourmet palate.

When you open a jar of Pind & Pipali, you aren't just tasting a condiment. You are tasting the soil of Punjab, the warmth of a Ludhiana summer, and the Maa de Hath da Swad that remains unchanged by time.

The Craft

The Sun-Drying Process

Every jar takes weeks, not hours. Here is why that matters.

01

Harvest & Selection

Only the finest seasonal produce makes the cut. Each Ramkela mango is hand-inspected for ripeness, firmness, and fragrance before it enters a Pind & Pipali jar.

02

Stone-Ground Masala

Our spice master grinds each batch fresh on a traditional silbatta. No machine grinding â€” the stone releases aromatic oils that bottling can't replicate.

03

Barni Curing

Packed into hand-glazed ceramic barnis (matka pots), each batch is sealed with a muslin cloth tied with jute string â€” sealed in tradition.

04

21 Days of Punjabi Sun

The barnis sit in our open courtyard, turned by hand each morning to ensure even sun exposure. No industrial heaters. Only time, sunlight, and patience.

The Sun-Soaked Method

We don't use industrial heaters. Our pickles "cook" slowly in traditional ceramic barnis under the natural Punjabi sun. This is the only way to lock in that deep, nostalgic flavour.

Ludhiana's Finest Produce

We source every ingredient from local farmers we know by name. If the spice isn't vibrant and the oil isn't pure, it doesn't enter our jars. Simple as that.

No Shortcuts
